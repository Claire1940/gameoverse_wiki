<Section icon={Target} title="The Rules of the Gameoverse">

The pilot introduces a fascinating set of rules that govern how these worlds operate. Every game world has a "Hero" and a "Villain." If the Hero defeats the Villain, the game is "cleared," and the world essentially resets or explodes into "Flux" (crystals). Facade’s mission is surprisingly counter-intuitive: they must ensure the Hero wins to collect these crystals, even if it means the destruction of the game world and its inhabitants.

  <Step num="1" title="Scouting Phase">

  <Step num="2" title="Intervention">
    Ensure the Hero stays on track and doesn't get distracted by "useless side quests."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Final Boss Fight">
    Support the Hero (often from the shadows) to ensure the Villain is defeated.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Flux Harvesting">
    Collect the crystalline remains of the world once the "Game Clear" screen appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This creates a moral dilemma for Kai, who realizes that "saving" a world by helping the hero actually leads to that world's ultimate demise. The pilot highlights this during the "Coral Reef City" arc, where Flappers the Dolphin is manipulated into defeating Snappy the Shark, leading to a catastrophic planetary explosion.
