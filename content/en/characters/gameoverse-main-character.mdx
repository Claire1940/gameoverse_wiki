<Section icon={Swords} title="The Gameoverse Mission: Preventing World Explosions">

The core premise of Gameoverse revolves around preventing game worlds from "exploding." This catastrophic event occurs when a game's hero successfully defeats its villain, leading to the termination of that game world. Kai and Gabb's objective, as Farcade operatives, is to prevent this outcome. However, their methods often diverge from the organization's cold, calculated approach.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Reconnaissance">
    Kai and Gabb are dispatched to an unknown game world to scan for heroes and villains. This initial phase is meant to be purely observational.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identifying Key Players">
    They must identify the game's designated hero and villain. This often involves interacting with game characters and understanding their roles within the narrative.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Intervention Strategy">
    The goal is to prevent the hero from achieving their objective (defeating the villain). This can involve subtle interference or, as Kai often prefers, direct assistance to the "villain" or even the hero, depending on her moral judgment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Managing Consequences">
    Interfering with game worlds carries significant risks, including attracting the attention of rival factions like Synthase or inadvertently causing greater instability. Kai must constantly adapt to unforeseen consequences.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Reporting to Farcade">
    After the mission, a report is due to Farcade, detailing the outcome and any anomalies. Kai's reports often highlight her deviations from protocol, creating tension with her superiors.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Their mission is complicated by the presence of Synthase, a rival organization that seeks to harvest "Floats" from exploding game worlds. This puts Kai and Gabb in direct conflict with Synthase agents, who actively try to cause game worlds to end for their own gain.

<Callout type="warning" title="The "Game Over" Consequence">
If a game's hero successfully defeats its villain, the game world "explodes," and all its inhabitants vanish. Kai's overarching mission is to prevent this from happening, even if it means altering the game's intended outcome.
</Callout>
