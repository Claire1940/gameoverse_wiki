<Section icon={Target} title="Understanding the Game Over Mechanic">

The core conflict in the series is based on the logic of 90s and 2000s video games. In the **gameoverse barney** world, "winning" is the ultimate disaster. This subversion of traditional gaming tropes creates a high-stakes environment where the heroes must act as "villains" to keep everyone alive.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Win Condition">
    Every world has a Hero and a Villain. The Hero is programmed to defeat the Villain, which triggers the "Game Over" sequence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Deletion Wave">
    Once the Win Condition is met (e.g., Gobbles collecting all vowels), the world is flagged as "Complete" and deleted from the system.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bypassing System Locks">
    Outsiders cannot directly harm residents due to a "System Lock." Farcade must use covert manipulation to ensure the Hero fails.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resource Harvesting">
    Syntax attempts to accelerate this process, using characters like Fold (cheat sheets) to help Heroes win faster.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="System Lock Penalty">
If an outsider reveals the nature of the Gameoverse to a resident, time stops for that world. Beams of light sweep the area to remove the "glitch" (the outsider), as seen when Kit panics in the pilot.
</Callout>

</Section>
