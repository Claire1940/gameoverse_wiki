<Section icon={Target} title="Optimizing In-Game Settings for Performance">
The most immediate and impactful way to improve your Gameoverse framerate is by adjusting the in-game graphics settings. Gameoverse offers a wide range of options that can be tweaked to balance visual quality and performance. Experimentation is key, as different systems will react differently to specific settings.

<Callout type="warning" title="Beware of "Ultra" Settings">
While "Ultra" settings promise the best visuals, they often come with a significant performance cost. Unless you have a top-tier gaming rig, aiming for a balance between "High" and "Medium" settings is usually the most effective approach for a stable framerate.
</Callout>

### Recommended Settings Adjustments

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lower Resolution">
    Reducing the display resolution is one of the most effective ways to boost FPS. A 1080p resolution (1920x1080) is often a good compromise between visual clarity and performance. If your monitor is 1440p or 4K, consider dropping down to 1080p for a substantial FPS gain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Texture Quality">
    Texture quality affects the detail of surfaces in the game. While high textures look great, they consume a lot of VRAM. If you have a graphics card with less than 8GB of VRAM, consider setting this to "Medium" or "High" instead of "Ultra."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reduce Shadow Quality">
    Shadows are notoriously demanding on GPUs. Lowering shadow quality or even disabling some shadow effects can provide a significant framerate increase with a relatively minor visual impact.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Disable Anti-Aliasing (AA)">
    Anti-aliasing smooths jagged edges but can be very resource-intensive. Try disabling it or using a less demanding form like FXAA instead of MSAA. Modern games often have good temporal anti-aliasing (TSSAA) that offers a good balance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Lower View Distance/Draw Distance">
    This setting determines how far into the distance objects are rendered. Reducing it can improve FPS, especially in open-world areas, but may cause objects to pop into view unnaturally.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Driver Settings Recommendations

Most graphics card control panels (NVIDIA Control Panel, AMD Radeon Software) offer additional settings that can be tweaked for performance.

| Setting (NVIDIA/AMD)  | Recommended Value   | Benefit                                         |
| :-------------------- | :------------------ | :---------------------------------------------- |
| **Image Sharpening**  | Off                 | Prevents additional rendering overhead          |
| **Low Latency Mode**  | Ultra / Enabled     | Reduces input lag, potentially improves responsiveness |
| **Power Management**  | Prefer Maximum Performance | Ensures GPU operates at full potential          |
| **Texture Filtering Quality** | Performance / High Performance | Prioritizes FPS over texture detail             |

### Advanced Optimization Strategies

1.  **Overclocking:** Carefully overclocking your CPU and GPU can yield significant performance gains, but it carries risks and should only be attempted if you understand the process and potential consequences. Always research and follow reputable guides.
2.  **RAM Speed:** Faster RAM (e.g., DDR4-3200MHz or higher) can improve CPU performance, particularly in CPU-bound scenarios. Ensure your RAM is running at its advertised speed via your motherboard's BIOS/UEFI settings.
3.  **Future Hardware Upgrades:** If all software optimizations fail to provide satisfactory results, a hardware upgrade might be necessary. Prioritize upgrading your graphics card first, followed by your CPU and then RAM, based on your budget and current system bottlenecks.
